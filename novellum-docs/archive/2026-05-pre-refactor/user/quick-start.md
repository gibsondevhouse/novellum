# Quick Start: Your First Project in 5 Minutes

This guide walks you through everything you need to get from a blank screen to your first written scene in Novellum. No prior experience needed.

---

## Step 1: Complete the Onboarding Flow

The first time you open Novellum, the onboarding screen greets you. It takes about a minute to complete:

1. **Enter your author name.** This is used as the default author field in exports.
2. **Review your data location.** Novellum shows you exactly where your projects will be saved. You do not need to change this.
3. **Skip or configure AI.** If you have an OpenRouter API key, you can add it now. If not, click **Skip** — you can add it later in **Settings → AI**.

After completing onboarding, you land on the **Project Hub**.

---

## Step 2: Create Your First Project

From the Project Hub, click the **New Project** button (or the large **+** card).

A dialog appears asking for:

- **Title** — the working title of your novel (you can change this later).
- **Genre** — choose the genre that best fits your story.
- **Synopsis** — a short description of your book. Even a single sentence is enough to start. This helps the AI assistant provide better suggestions later.

Click **Create** when ready. Novellum creates a new project and opens it immediately.

---

## Step 3: Add Your First Scene in the Outline

You are now in the project editor. The left panel shows the **Outline** — a hierarchical structure of your story organised into Acts, Chapters, and Scenes.

A default structure is created for you. To add your first scene:

1. Click the **+** icon next to a Chapter, or right-click a Chapter and choose **Add Scene**.
2. Give the scene a short title, such as "Opening scene" or "Chapter 1, Scene 1".
3. Press **Enter** or click **Create**.

Your scene now appears in the outline tree.

---

## Step 4: Write in the Editor

Click your scene in the outline to open it in the **Editor** panel on the right.

The editor is a clean, distraction-free writing surface. Click anywhere in the editor area and start typing. There is no mode to enter — just write.

A few things to know as you write:

- **Word count** appears in the editor toolbar and updates as you type.
- **Formatting** (bold, italic, headings) is accessible via the toolbar at the top of the editor, or via keyboard shortcuts.
- **Focus Mode** hides the outline panel so you see only the editor. Press **Cmd+Shift+F** (macOS) or **Ctrl+Shift+F** (Windows) to toggle it.

---

## Step 5: Saving Is Automatic

Novellum saves your work automatically as you type. There is no **Save** button to click and no risk of losing your work if the app closes unexpectedly.

You can verify that your work is saved by looking at the title bar — it will not show any unsaved indicator.

If you want to trigger a manual save explicitly (for example, before closing the lid of your laptop), press **Cmd+S** (macOS) or **Ctrl+S** (Windows). The editor confirms the save with a brief indicator in the toolbar.

---

## Step 6: Export Your Manuscript

When you are ready to share or submit your work, use the **Export** feature:

1. Click the **Export** button in the top-right toolbar of the project editor, or press **Cmd+E** (macOS) / **Ctrl+E** (Windows).
2. Choose an export profile:
   - **Standard Manuscript** — formatted for literary agent submission.
   - **Reader Copy** — a clean reading copy.
   - **Ebook Draft** — EPUB-optimised with table of contents.
   - **Plain Text Archive** — minimal Markdown, no formatting.
3. Fill in your manuscript metadata (title, author, subtitle).
4. Click **Export** and choose where to save the file.

The exported file is saved to your Downloads folder (or wherever you specify).
