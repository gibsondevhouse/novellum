# Stage-006: World-Building and Continuity

## Goal

Turn world-building, entity detail, continuity, redirect, and future-state surfaces into production-ready dossier systems. The deep hierarchy must feel intentional and navigable rather than scattered.

## Entry Criteria

- Stage 005 project hub treatment is stable.
- Seed fixture includes representative characters, relationships, locations, lore, plot threads, timeline events, and continuity issues.
- Shared poster, tile, empty-state, drawer, and command primitives are available.

## Phases

| # | Phase | Status | Owner | Est. Duration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 001 | [World-Building Domain Overview](phase-001-world-overview/phase-001-world-overview.md) | `complete` | Architect / Stylist | 7d |

## Required Deliverables

- `/world-building` presents Personae, Atlas, Archive, Threads, and Chronicles as distinct cinematic domains.
- Section landing pages use consistent domain shelf/tile treatment.
- Entity collections use the right pattern for the workflow: poster grids, visual tiles, timelines, dossiers, or dense tables.
- Character/entity details feel like dossiers with visual anchors and structured panels.
- `/continuity` provides issue triage with severity/status grouping, review actions, prompt/style surfaces, and polished empty states.
- Legacy bible and consistency redirects do not expose rough blank pages.
- Arc future-state pages are polished and navigable.

## Exit Criteria

- Every world-building domain has a clear first-viewport focal point.
- Entity detail pages remain productive forms while gaining cinematic dossier structure.
- Continuity issue states are keyboard-accessible and not color-only.
- World-building and continuity seeded screenshots pass review.

## Notes

Do not flatten the semantic hierarchy. The production UI should clarify the meaning of Personae, Atlas, Archive, Threads, and Chronicles while preserving fast navigation for large projects.
